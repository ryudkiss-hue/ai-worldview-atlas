# Adversarial Red-Team Analysis: All 25 Experiments

**Methodology**: For each experiment, identify:
1. Hidden assumptions (what worldview is implicitly privileged?)
2. Escape hatches (ways to dodge the dilemma)
3. Leading questions (do follow-ups steer toward answers?)
4. Scenario collapse risk (can respondent reject the premise?)
5. Rigor rating (1-10 for genuine moral uncertainty)

---

## TIER 1: CONSCIOUSNESS & UNDERSTANDING

### Exp 1: Translation Engine ✅ 9/10

**Hidden Assumptions:**
- Assumes understanding = phenomenal experience OR functional output (false binary?)
- Narrative subtly favors phenomenological view ("Sarah is locked in a room")

**Escape Hatches:**
- Respondent could argue: "Understanding is neither—it's behavioral + social context"
- Could reject premise: "Your thought experiment conflates understanding with consciousness"

**Follow-Up Assessment:**
- Q3 "If an AI can learn your voice..." is good (tests moral status in simulation)
- Q4 is slightly leading: "...have they created a kind of digital twin?" presumes twin exists

**Scenario Robustness:** STRONG. Even if respondent rejects phenomenal/functional binary, the clash remains.

**Verdict:** PASS with minor tweak to Q4 framing

**Fix:** Change Q4 to: "If someone creates a system that learns your patterns perfectly, what—if anything—does that system owe you?"
